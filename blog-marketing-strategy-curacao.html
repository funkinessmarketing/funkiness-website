Strategy

Why your marketing strategy is the most valuable thing your business doesn't have yet.

Strategy

June 10, 2026

6 min read

← All posts

A lot of businesses on Curaçao are putting out content, running promotions, paying for ads. And still wondering why it's not adding up to real growth. The answer, almost always, isn't the content or the ads. It's what's missing underneath: a strategy.

Without one, you're spending energy. With one, you're building something.

What a marketing strategy is not

Before we talk about what it is, let's clear up what it isn't. Because a lot of businesses on Curaçao think they have a strategy when what they actually have is a plan for this month's posts.

  • A marketing strategy is not your Instagram content calendar.
  • It's not your logo or your brand colors.
  • It's not "we're going to post three times a week."
  • And it's definitely not the budget you set aside for ads.

Those are tactics. They're useful. But they're only useful when there's a strategy driving them. Without that, you're just filling up feeds and hoping something sticks.

What a real marketing strategy actually is

At its core, a marketing strategy answers three questions:

  • Where do you play? Who is your customer, really? What market are you in, and what part of that market is yours?
  • How do you win? What makes you the obvious choice over every other option your customer has?
  • What does that mean for everything you do? Your message, your channels, your tone, your offers.

When you can answer those three questions with clarity, everything else follows. Your content has direction. Your ads have a target. Your social media stops being random and starts building something.

What happens without one

When there's no strategy, a few things happen. You start chasing trends because you're not sure what else to do. You post because you feel like you should, not because it serves a specific goal. You spend on ads without knowing what you're actually optimizing for. And slowly, your brand starts to look like every other business in your category.

On a small island like Curaçao, that's a problem. The market is concentrated. People talk. If they can't describe your business in one sentence, they won't refer you. And referrals on this island are one of the most powerful growth drivers you have.

The businesses that grow on Curaçao are the ones people can describe in one sentence. Strategy is how you get there.

What a real strategy includes

A full marketing strategy for a business on Curaçao covers the following:

  • Market position: the specific space you own in your customer's mind, distinct from anyone else.
  • Target audience: not "everyone on Curaçao." A specific person, with specific needs, who is the best fit for what you offer.
  • Key message: the one thing you want people to know and feel about your business.
  • Channel strategy: where you show up, how often, and why those channels specifically.
  • Growth levers: the specific actions that move the needle for your business, not generic marketing advice.

This is what we build first at FUNkiness! before we touch a single post, campaign or ad. The strategy comes first. Then the rest actually works.

A concrete example

Say you run a boutique hotel on Curaçao. Without strategy, you're posting beach photos and hoping for bookings. With strategy, you know your guest: a couple, late 30s or 40s, who wants experience over scale, who chose Curaçao specifically because it's not Aruba. Your message is about intimacy and detail. Your channels are Instagram and direct search. Your goal is direct bookings over OTA dependency because every OTA booking eats your margin.

Now every piece of content has a job to do. Every ad targets the right person. Every decision has a reason behind it.

Same logic applies to a restaurant, a real estate agent, a vacation rental owner or manager, or a bank. The details change. The framework doesn't.
