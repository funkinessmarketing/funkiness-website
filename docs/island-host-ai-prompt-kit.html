01
Airbnb Listing Description
Airbnb has an automatic translation feature - but it's generic and you have zero control over the result. Turn it off. Instead, go to your listing settings and manually add your own text per language. Dutch guests then see your Dutch version. American and Canadian guests see your English version. Both run on the same listing at the same time. This is what these outputs are for: copy each one separately and paste it into the right language field yourself. The "Your Space" / "Je accommodatie" field works the same way - Outputs 3 and 4 give you both languages for that field too. Most hosts never do this. That's why their listings read like a machine wrote them.
Output 1 - Dutch market (in Dutch) → upload as Dutch translation
You are a top-tier hospitality copywriter specializing in Caribbean vacation rentals. Write an Airbnb listing description for my property for the Dutch market. Max 400 words. Write in Dutch (Nederlands). No clichés: no "paradijs," "parel," "home away from home," or "adembenemend." Nederlandse lezers prikken direct door overdreven marketing heen. Open niet met generieke eilandsfeer. Begin met wat specifiek en oprecht is aan mijn woning. Benadruk waarde, authenticiteit en hoe een verblijf hier dag-tot-dag aanvoelt. Geen superlatieven. Geen "je zult verliefd worden." Schrijf voor iemand die vandaag al twintig listings heeft bekeken en meteen doorheeft wanneer iets klinkt als een template.
Output 2 - American market (in English) → upload as English (US) translation
You are a top-tier hospitality copywriter specializing in Caribbean vacation rentals. Write an Airbnb listing description for my property for the American market. Max 500 words. Write in English. No clichés: no "paradise," "gem," "home away from home," or "stunning." American readers respond to aspirational, experience-driven copy. Open with a scene that makes them feel the island. Build desire for the experience. Make a case for why this island over other Caribbean options - position it as the smarter, less crowded, more authentic alternative. Frame the property as the obvious choice for their trip. Use second person ("you'll wake up to..."). Aspirational but specific - vague luxury language loses them.
Output 3 - Canadian market (in English) → upload as English (Canada) translation
You are a top-tier hospitality copywriter specializing in Caribbean vacation rentals. Write an Airbnb listing description for my property for the Canadian market. Max 500 words. Write in English. No clichés: no "paradise," "gem," "home away from home," or "stunning." Canadian guests are escaping real winters. Canada's major cities - Toronto, Montreal, Calgary, Vancouver - experience months of cold, snow and grey skies. Lead with the contrast: what it actually feels like to step off a plane here when it's -20°C back home. Frame the island and this property as the antidote to a Canadian winter. This is the most concrete and emotionally resonant booking trigger for this market. Close with a direct call to action that makes booking feel like the obvious decision before winter hits.
Output 4 - Your Space in English → upload as English translation
Write the "Your Space" section for my Airbnb listing. This is a separate field specifically about the physical property. Max 300 words. Write in English. Describe each area briefly but vividly: living area, kitchen, bedrooms, bathrooms, outdoor space. Focus on what guests actually use and remember. Be specific about standout features. Warm and honest tone.
Output 5 - Je accommodatie in het Nederlands → upload as Dutch translation
Schrijf de "Je accommodatie" sectie voor mijn Airbnb listing. Dit is een apart veld specifiek over de fysieke woning. Max 300 woorden. Schrijf in het Nederlands. Beschrijf elke ruimte kort maar levendig: woonkamer, keuken, slaapkamers, badkamers, buitenruimte. Focus op wat gasten daadwerkelijk gebruiken en onthouden. Wees specifiek over opvallende kenmerken. Warme en eerlijke toon.

Seasonal Listing Update
Seasonal listing updates signal to Airbnb's algorithm that your listing is actively managed, which can lift your search ranking during competitive periods. Post them 3-4 weeks before the season starts to capture early bookers before the market fills up.
Write a short listing update notice (max 100 words) to add to the top of my vacation rental description for [SEASON/PERIOD]. Make it feel exciting, not administrative. Details: - Period: [e.g. Carnival season Curacao / dive season Bonaire / high season Aruba / December-January / Easter week] - What makes this time of year special on [ISLAND]: [BE SPECIFIC: events, weather, what guests can experience] - Availability status: [A FEW DATES LEFT / FILLING UP / FULLY BOOKED EXCEPT FOR X DATES] Write for a guest who is still deciding whether to book this particular period. Make the season itself feel like something worth planning around. No "don't miss out" or "act fast." Urgency through specificity, not pressure.

VRBO Description
VRBO allows up to 10,000 characters - far more room than Airbnb. Use it to be thorough, not to pad. Unlike Airbnb, VRBO does not support separate descriptions per language - your listing shows the same text to everyone. Choose the version that matches your primary audience: Output 1 (Dutch) if most of your guests are European, Output 2 (English) if you primarily target American or Canadian guests.
Output 1 - Dutch / European market (in Dutch)
You are a vacation home copywriter specializing in Caribbean vacation rentals. Write a VRBO listing description for my property for the Dutch and European market. Target 600-800 words. Write in Dutch (Nederlands). No clichés: no "paradijs," "parel," "adembenemend" or "home away from home." Nederlandse lezers vergelijken listings zorgvuldig en prikken meteen door een template heen. Begin met wat specifiek en oprecht is aan deze woning. Benadruk waarde, praktische kwaliteiten en hoe een verblijf hier dag-tot-dag aanvoelt. Geen superlatieven. Geen inspirerende opening. Schrijf voor iemand die vandaag al twintig listings heeft bekeken en een reden zoekt om deze te vertrouwen. VRBO staat maximaal 10.000 tekens toe - gebruik die ruimte om grondig te zijn en elke vraag te beantwoorden, niet om de tekst op te vullen.
Output 2 - North American market (in English)
You are a vacation home copywriter specializing in Caribbean vacation rentals. Write a VRBO listing description for my property for the North American market. Target 600-800 words. Write in English. No clichés: no "paradise," "gem," "stunning" or "home away from home." North American readers respond to aspirational, experience-driven copy. Open with a scene-setting sentence (morning coffee by the pool, sunset on the terrace). Use second person throughout: "you'll wake up to..." Include one paragraph on why this island is worth choosing over other Caribbean options. Close with what makes this specific home worth booking over every other option they've seen today. Aspirational but specific - vague luxury language loses them. VRBO allows up to 10,000 characters - use the space to build a complete picture, not just list features.

Nog één aanvullende vraag voordat je begint: Wat maakt deze woning anders dan alle andere opties op dit eiland? [WEES SPECIFIEK: het ene element waarmee jij je onderscheidt van andere verhuurders op het eiland] Schrijf in het Nederlands. Toon: warm, eerlijk en persoonlijk, met een vleugje avontuur. Listing Title Optimizer
Your listing title is searchable on Airbnb and VRBO. Airbnb gives you 50 characters; VRBO gives you 80. Most hosts waste this space on the property name. Guests filter by keywords like "pool," "ocean view" and "beachfront." This prompt fills that space correctly.
You are a vacation rental SEO and conversion specialist. Write optimized listing titles for my property on the ABC islands for multiple platforms. You already have my property details. One question: what are my top 3 most searchable features? [e.g. private pool, ocean view, walk to beach - the things guests actually search for] Write optimized titles for: Airbnb (max 50 characters): lead with the most searchable feature, not the property name. Include the property name only if space allows. VRBO (max 80 characters): more room to add a second benefit or the location. Direct website / Google: no character limit. Write for a guest who just searched "vacation rental [ISLAND] with pool." Give 2 variations per platform. Explain in one sentence which keyword you prioritized for each and why.
06
Short Teaser Description
Short descriptions appear in link previews, social media bios, email signatures and Google results. Most hosts paste their full listing description here and wonder why it doesn't convert. Two sharp sentences beat four paragraphs every time.
Write 3 versions of a 2-sentence teaser description for my vacation rental. You already have my property details. Each version takes a completely different angle. Sensory language only. No clichés. No questions. No exclamation marks. No "paradise." To help you nail the tone, tell me: Strongest feature: [e.g. private infinity pool facing west for sunsets] Second feature: [e.g. 4-minute walk to Klein Knip beach] Vibe: [e.g. relaxed and private / family-friendly / romantic / digital nomad] Version A: lead with the feeling of arriving here for the first time Version B: lead with the location and what surrounds the property Version C: lead with the specific feature that no other rental nearby can match After the 3 versions, tell me in one sentence which one you'd recommend for an Airbnb link preview and why.

Rewrite Existing Description
A listing description written 18 months ago is probably costing you bookings. Writing styles shift, platforms change how they display text, and your own understanding of your guests deepens over time. This rewrite prompt removes everything that could describe any property anywhere.
You are a senior hospitality copywriter. Rewrite my vacation rental listing description. Remove every cliché, vague adjective and generic statement that could describe any property anywhere in the world. Every sentence must be specific to this property on [ISLAND]. My current description: [PASTE YOUR CURRENT DESCRIPTION HERE] Things I should have included but didn't: [ADD DETAILS: specific views, local touches, things guests always comment on, unique features you take for granted] Platform this is for: [AIRBNB / VRBO / MICAZU / DIRECT WEBSITE] Rules for the rewrite: - Show, don't tell. Replace "beautiful view" with what makes it beautiful. - Lead with the strongest sentence. Cut the slow warm-up. - Keep it the same length or shorter. - End with a line that makes the reader want to look at the photos next. After the rewrite, list the 3 specific changes you made and why each one improves conversion.
08
Unique Selling Points
Most vacation rental listings describe the same things in the same order. Pool, beach distance, kitchen, WiFi. This prompt finds what is genuinely different about your property and turns it into language that sticks.
I need help identifying and writing the unique selling points of my vacation rental. You already have my property details. Be brutally honest: if it isn't actually unique, cut it. Three things I want you to factor in that aren't in my property info: - Something that surprises guests when they arrive: [THE THING PHOTOS DON'T CAPTURE] - Price compared to similar properties here: [CHEAPER / SIMILAR / MORE EXPENSIVE] - Who this property is NOT right for: [BE HONEST] Deliver: 1. Five bullet points, max 15 words each. Each one must survive this test: "Could another rental on this island say the exact same thing?" If yes, rewrite it. 2. One headline sentence (max 12 words) that captures the total appeal of this property. 3. One honest sentence about who this property is NOT for. (This increases trust and reduces bad-fit bookings.)
09
Pool and Outdoor Space
On tropical islands, outdoor spaces sell rentals more than any interior feature. Yet most descriptions spend half their words on the living room. If your property has a private pool or terrace, this paragraph should appear early in your listing, not at the end.
Write a vivid, sensory paragraph (100-150 words) describing the outdoor space of my vacation rental on [ISLAND]. Make the reader feel like they're already there. No clichés. Specific details only. What my outdoor space includes: [LIST EVERYTHING: pool, sun loungers, outdoor shower, BBQ, terrace, garden, sea view, palm trees, hammock, outdoor dining table] Orientation: [faces east/west/south / morning sun / afternoon shade / sunset view] Size and feel: [compact and intimate / spacious / sprawling / private and hidden / open and social] The detail guests always comment on: [WHAT DO PEOPLE ALWAYS MENTION ABOUT THE OUTDOOR SPACE?] Write in second person ("you"). Open with the exact moment the reader steps outside. End with the thing they will not want to leave. Do not describe the pool dimensions. Describe the feeling of being in it.
10
Location Description
Saying "5 minutes from the beach" is less persuasive than walking the reader through the neighborhood. Distance in minutes is data. A sense of place is desire. This prompt makes your location feel like a character, not a coordinate.
Write a location section for my vacation rental listing that makes [NEIGHBORHOOD] on [ISLAND] feel like a place worth choosing, not just a geographic fact. Location details: - Neighborhood name and character: [e.g. "Jan Thiel, quiet and residential with a small marina nearby"] - What you can reach on foot: [BEACH / RESTAURANTS / SUPERMARKET / TOWN / LIST THEM] - What's within 10 minutes by car: [LIST 3-4 PLACES WITH WHAT MAKES EACH WORTH VISITING] - The local spot most tourists never find: [YOUR ACTUAL INSIDER RECOMMENDATION] - Who loves this location most: [COUPLES / FAMILIES / DIVERS / REMOTE WORKERS / etc.] Write 2 paragraphs. First: the feel, rhythm and personality of this neighborhood. Make it specific to [ISLAND]. Second: practical proximity with warmth. Never list distances like a real estate brochure. Describe what the walk or drive actually feels like.

11
5-Star Review Response
Never open with "Thank you for your wonderful review." Future guests have read that 200 times and it tells them nothing. A response that references what the guest actually said signals that you pay attention, which is exactly what future guests want to know.
Write a response to this 5-star guest review. The tone should feel personal and specific, not templated. Future guests reading this response should feel that this host genuinely cares. My name: [YOUR NAME] Property: [PROPERTY NAME] on [ISLAND] Guest review: [PASTE THE FULL REVIEW HERE] Rules: - Do not open with "Thank you for your wonderful review" or any variation of it - Reference at least 2 specific things the guest mentioned - Add one sentence about what made their visit particularly memorable to you as a host - Invite them back with one specific reason to return (a season, an event, something new you've added) - Max 100 words - The last sentence should not be "We hope to see you again soon"
12
Positive Review With Minor Complaint
How you handle small criticism tells future guests more about you as a host than a perfect 5-star response ever could. Acknowledge it directly, show what you did about it, and move on. Over-apologizing makes it feel bigger than it is.
A guest left a positive review but mentioned one minor issue. Write a response that acknowledges it without amplifying it, while reinforcing the overall positive experience. My name: [YOUR NAME] / Property: [PROPERTY NAME] Guest review: [PASTE REVIEW] The issue mentioned: [DESCRIBE IT] What you've done about it since: [e.g. fixed the AC unit / added an extra fan / improved the WiFi router / repainted the gate] Tone: confident, warm, and solution-focused. Never defensive. The response should leave a future guest thinking "good host" not "I wonder if that issue is still there." Max 120 words.
13
Neutral 3-Star Review
A 3-star response is read more carefully by future guests than a 5-star response. They are specifically looking for how you react to criticism. Write it as a reassurance letter to the next person reading it, not as a reply to the reviewer.
A guest left a 3-star review. Write a calm, professional response that shows I take feedback seriously, addresses the specific points raised, and reassures future guests. My name: [YOUR NAME] / Property: [PROPERTY NAME] Guest review: [PASTE REVIEW] Context to inform tone (do not include this directly in the response): [WAS THE COMPLAINT FAIR? WAS THERE MISCOMMUNICATION? WHAT REALLY HAPPENED?] Rules: - Acknowledge the experience without dismissing it - Address each point raised with one clear sentence - Do not over-apologize or justify at length - Close by welcoming future guests who value what this property genuinely offers - Max 130 words - Write for the next 1,000 people who will read this, not for the guest who left it
14
Unfair or Exaggerated Negative Review
Write this for the 97% of future guests who read it, not for the 3% who leave unfair reviews. Staying calm and factual does more for your reputation than any rebuttal. The goal is to reassure the reader, not to win an argument.
A guest left an unfair or inaccurate review. Write a calm, professional response that gently corrects the record without sounding defensive or emotional. My name: [YOUR NAME] / Property: [PROPERTY NAME] Guest review: [PASTE REVIEW] What actually happened (use this to inform the response, not to copy verbatim): [EXPLAIN THE REAL SITUATION AS CALMLY AS POSSIBLE] Rules: - Stay 100% professional throughout - Present the facts clearly without attacking the guest - Acknowledge any kernel of truth, even if small - Never mention refunds, disputes, platform policies or threats - The final sentence should make a future guest feel confident booking - Max 150 words

Review Response in Dutch

16
Review Response in Spanish or Papiamentu
Responding in a guest's language is one of the highest-return gestures in hospitality. It signals respect and dramatically increases the likelihood they return, refer friends, or leave an even better review on their next stay.
Write a response to this guest review in [SPANISH / PAPIAMENTU] for my vacation rental on [ISLAND]. My name: [YOUR NAME] / Property: [PROPERTY NAME] Guest review: [PASTE REVIEW] Language: [SPANISH / PAPIAMENTU] If writing in Papiamentu: use [CURACAO / ARUBA / BONAIRE] spelling conventions. Keep it natural and local. Avoid overly formal or textbook phrasing. It should sound like a real person on the island wrote it, not a translation tool. Reference at least one specific thing the guest mentioned. Max 100 words.
17
Returning Guest Review Response
A returning guest is your most powerful social proof. Their review says something no first-timer can say: "I came back." A personalized response to that fact works as free marketing that sits on your listing for years.
A guest who has stayed at my property before left a review mentioning they returned. Write a response that makes them feel like part of the story of this property. My name: [YOUR NAME] / Property: [PROPERTY NAME] How many times have they stayed: [NUMBER] Guest review: [PASTE REVIEW] Anything specific you remember about their previous stays (optional): [ADD IF RELEVANT] The response should acknowledge their loyalty in a way that feels genuine, not like a hotel loyalty program. Make them feel like they have a place here. Invite them back with a specific reason. Max 100 words.
18
Review Mentioning a Specific Issue
Address the issue directly and early. Vagueness invites future guests to imagine the worst. One clear sentence explaining what you did about it is more reassuring than three sentences of general apology.
A guest mentioned a specific issue in their review. Write a response that addresses it clearly, shows what's been done, and reassures future guests without over-explaining. Issue type: [NOISE / CLEANLINESS / WIFI / AC / PARKING / MAINTENANCE / OTHER: specify] What you've done: [SPECIFIC FIX: e.g. "replaced the AC unit in July" / "added a second WiFi router" / "hired a new cleaning team" - or honest context if ongoing] Guest review: [PASTE REVIEW] My name: [YOUR NAME] / Property: [PROPERTY NAME] Tone: honest, accountable and solution-focused. Do not hedge or over-explain. Future guests reading this should finish it feeling reassured, not uncertain. Max 130 words.
Category 02 - Review Responses19-20
19
Asking a Guest to Update Their Review
The window to ask for a review update is 48-72 hours after resolving the issue. Long enough to feel genuine, short enough that the stay is still vivid. Frame it as optional and earned, never as an expectation. Important: do not use this on Airbnb. Airbnb explicitly prohibits asking guests to change or remove a review - it can result in an account warning or suspension, regardless of how friendly the message is. This prompt is for Booking.com, VRBO and direct bookings only.
A guest left a review after an issue arose, but we resolved it well and they seemed happy with how I handled it. Write a short, non-pushy private message asking if they'd consider updating their review. Platform: [BOOKING.COM / VRBO / DIRECT BOOKING] What the issue was and how you resolved it: [DESCRIBE BOTH CLEARLY] How the guest reacted when the issue was resolved: [DID THEY THANK YOU? SEEM SATISFIED? SAY ANYTHING?] My name: [YOUR NAME] Rules: - Never pressure, bribe or imply you are owed an update - Frame it as entirely optional - mean it - Keep it short: the shorter it is, the more genuine it sounds - Max 80 words
20
Turn a Rave Review Into a Marketing Quote
Your best reviews are your best marketing copy. Most hosts leave them sitting on the booking platform when they should be on their Instagram bio, website homepage and email signature. This prompt turns raw guest language into polished, usable quotes.
A guest left me a great review and I want to repurpose it as marketing copy. Edit it into 3 polished versions for different uses. Original review: [PASTE FULL REVIEW] Guest's first name or initials: [NAME / INITIALS] Where they're from (if you know): [COUNTRY] Version A: Full testimonial quote (max 40 words). Formatted for a website or listing homepage. Tighten the original without changing the meaning. Version B: Short pull-quote (max 15 words). One punchy sentence for an Instagram caption, story, or highlight cover. Version C: Caption-ready version. The quote formatted for an Instagram post, with a suggested 1-line intro before the quote and 5 relevant hashtags for a vacation rental on [ISLAND] at the end.

Instagram Property Photo Caption
Instagram shows the first line of your caption before the "more" button. If that line doesn't stop the scroll, nothing else matters. These 3 variations give you angles to test: feeling, detail and counter-intuitive hook.
Write 3 Instagram captions for a photo of my vacation rental on [ISLAND]. Each takes a completely different angle. No travel clichés. No "paradise." No "stunning." Photo description: [DESCRIBE EXACTLY WHAT'S IN THE PHOTO: light, angle, what's visible, time of day] Property: [NAME] / Target guest: [couples / families / divers / remote workers / etc.] Caption A: open with the emotion or feeling this photo creates. End with a soft CTA (link in bio, DM to book, or comment below).
