Airbnb

The largest vacation rental platform globally. On the ABC islands it drives the majority of international bookings. Your listing description and Your Space field are each limited to 500 characters: roughly 60-80 words. Only the first 295 characters show without "read more." Write for that first screen. Turn off auto-translation and set your own text per language to reach Dutch, American and Canadian guests in their own language.

Platform 02

VRBO

VRBO reaches primarily North American families and groups looking for full-home rentals. The platform gives you up to 10,000 characters for your description, far more than Airbnb. Unlike Airbnb, VRBO shows one listing to all guests regardless of language, so choose the version that fits your primary audience. VRBO guests tend to book longer stays and read listings more carefully than on other platforms.

VRBO02

VRBO Description
VRBO allows up to 10,000 characters, far more room than Airbnb. Use it to be thorough, not to pad. Unlike Airbnb, VRBO does not support separate descriptions per language, meaning your listing shows the same text to everyone. Choose the version that matches your primary audience: Output 1 (Dutch) if most of your guests are European, Output 2 (English) if you primarily target American or Canadian guests. Important: since April 2025, VRBO prohibits mentioning mandatory fees or taxes (resort fees, tourism tax, parking charges) in the listing description. Keep those in the pricing settings only, or your listing risks being flagged.

06
Unique Selling Points
Most listings on the ABC islands use the same 6 words: pool, view, beach, clean, spacious, well-equipped. This prompt forces a harder question: what can only you say? The test is blunt: if another host on the island could say the exact same thing, it doesn't count. What usually passes are the things the host takes for granted: the outdoor shower that faces the sea, the breadfruit tree guests always photograph, the way morning light hits the terrace. Those details convert. The generic ones don't.

Airbnb Algorithm Audit
Airbnb's search ranking is determined by more than 14 factors beyond your listing text. Response rate, listing completeness, photo quantity, amenities listed, Instant Book status, review velocity and calendar activity all feed the algorithm directly. Most hosts write a great description and stop there. This prompt audits what else needs attention.
You are an Airbnb optimization expert. Audit my listing against Airbnb's known ranking factors. Use my property details as context. Evaluate each factor as strong / needs work / unknown: 1. Listing completeness: every field filled in? 2. Photo quantity: at or above 20 photos? 3. Amenities: likely amenities I have but haven't listed? 4. Instant Book: enabled or not? 5. Response rate and speed: hitting the targets? 6. Review count and recency: is my review velocity healthy? 7. Pricing: competitive for my market and dates? 8. Calendar: updated regularly to signal active management? After the audit, give me a numbered action list ranked by expected impact on bookings. Be specific. No vague advice.

Listing Photo Brief
Photos are the #1 conversion factor on every rental platform. Guests decide in under 3 seconds whether to click. Your first photo is your billboard in search results. Airbnb recommends 20 or more photos. Most hosts upload 10-12. Every additional photo adds to your completeness score. Airbnb also supports a caption on each photo, visible when guests click through the gallery. Almost no one uses this. It is free copy space directly in front of a guest who is already considering your property.
